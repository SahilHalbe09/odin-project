Classic Cuban Midnight (Medianoche) Sandwich

Description

This sandwich is called a 'Media Noche' which translates to 'Midnight.' It is not exactly a "traditional" Cuban sandwich, for those of you fortunate to have had one, but this one makes a wonderful dinner sandwich because it is served hot. A nice side dish is black bean soup or black beans and rice, and plantain chips. COOKS NOTE: Bust out your Foreman Grill, or Panini Press! Please note: this is an adopted recipe and I hope to make it soonest.

Once the sandwiches are assembled, it's important to press them well while toasting them. If you have a panini press, you can use that. Otherwise, the easiest thing to do is to set a second heavy skillet on top of the first, and then press down on that. Only with enough pressure will you achieve the sandwich's signature compact, crisp look.

ingredients

  • 4 sweet bread rolls
  • ½ cup mayonnaise
  • ¼ cup prepared mustard
  • 1 pound thinly sliced cooked ham
  • 1 pound thinly sliced fully cooked pork
  • 1 pound sliced Swiss cheese
  • 1 cup dill pickle slices
  • 2 tablespoons butter, melted
